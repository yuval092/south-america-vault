# La Paz

The famous capital city and one of the most intriguing and authentic in the continent, standing at an altitude of 3,600 meters. A colorful city offering markets, shopping centers, museums, nightlife, and many attractions. Highlights include:
* Touring the touristic Llampu Street, located in the center of Israeli hostels.
* Shopping at the 'Blue Market' for custom-made leather jackets and fleeces.
* The Witches' Market, offering medicinal plants and potions.
* El Alto Market for clothing and electrical appliances (a huge market, so prepare time for the visit).
* The Thieves' Market, which sells stolen electronics.
* City attractions: The Cable Car (a popular mode of transport and a great way to see the city view), the Spider-Man attraction, Killi Killi viewpoint overlooking the city, Plaza Murillo (the central square housing the National Congress), San Pedro Prison tour, Tiwanaku ruins, Cholita Wrestling in El Alto (every Sunday), Coca Museum, Music Museum, San Miguel Church, and Multicine mall.
* Surrounding attractions and treks: Death Road biking, ATV tours in Moon Valley (can be taken from La Paz or rented on-site), El Choro Trek on the Inca Trail, Huayna Potosi Trek (reaching over 6,000 meters, difficult and unique), Sajama Trek (climb to the summit or a beautiful circuit), Condoriri Trek (mountain shaped like a condor, a difficult two-day trek), North and South Glacier Trek (ice climbing experience), Rio Verde Trek (includes building a raft and sailing, note that this is dangerous), Yunga Cruz ridge, sailing on the Tuichi River, and Chacaltaya Mountain viewpoint.
* Hard Rock Club is a favorite nightlife spot for tourists.
