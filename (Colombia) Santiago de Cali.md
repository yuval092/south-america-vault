# Santiago de Cali

A city in southwestern Colombia located in the Pacific region, considered one of the oldest cities in the Americas. San Fernando and San Antonio neighborhoods host most hostels. You can visit:
* City center - Plaza de Caicedo.
* Nightlife area.
* Main Cathedral of Cali.
* Sebastián de Belalcázar viewpoint overlooking the city.
* San Cristobal viewpoint (Cristo Rey) with a giant Jesus statue.
* Tardes Caleñas Rozo water park.
* City cable car.
* Skydiving.
* Flying ATV and gliding.
* Horseback riding.
* Bicycle tours in the Cali mountains.
* Visit the village and do kayaking/tubing in San Cipriano nature reserve - arrival by a special cart running on train tracks (bus from Cali to Buenaventura, get off at Zaragoza).
* Street tours and coffee tours.
* Botanical Garden.
* Palmetto Plaza mall and Centenario shopping center.
* Surroundings: Many green areas and nature reserves including Lake Calima (famous for kitesurfing) and whale watching on Pacific beaches a few hours away.
* Famous experience: Salsa lessons (free in hostels, or at local clubs/schools).
* Note: 'Opium and Marijuana fields tour' ended in tragedy years ago and Israelis avoid it.
* Tip: Free entry to Jewish Country Club Shalom upon presenting an Israeli passport (pools, tennis, golf, etc.).
