# Ushuaia

The southernmost city in the country, also serving as a transit point to Antarctica.

**Arrival:**
Arrival in Ushuaia by flight from Buenos Aires or Puerto Madryn (too many hours by bus) or flight/bus from Calafate.

**Tip:**
During peak season, book an exit ticket from Ushuaia upon arrival so you don't get stuck there for a few days. The city can be visited all year round, and in winter months the weather is quite stable and not too different from summer months.

**Attractions:**
* The 'Tierra del Fuego' National Park is considered a central destination when visiting Ushuaia
* Trail 1 Pampa Alta about a 2-hour hike
* Trail 2 Coastal Path about a 4-hour hike
* Trail 3 Hito along the lake about 4 hours
* Trail 4 Guanaco Viewpoint leads to a summit overlooking the entire park, about 8 hours
* The national park can also be visited by train (click for train website)
* Wildlife watching cruise
* Martial Glacier viewpoint
* Laguna Esmeralda which includes an easy trail partly in mud up to the lagoon
* Laguna de los Tempanos 'The Frozen Lagoon'
* The Submarine Waterfall hike
* The Sheep Trek
* Valdivieso Circuit Trek
* Navarino Teeth Trek (boat to Navarino Island in Chile and departure for trek)
* Knights Trek
* Five Brothers Trek (click for recommendation post)
* Cruise to Antarctica
